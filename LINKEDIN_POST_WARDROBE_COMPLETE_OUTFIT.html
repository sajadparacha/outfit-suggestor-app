This is my favourite feature in wardrob — and it didn’t arrive fully formed.

It started with a simple question: “What if I already know part of what I want to wear — can AI finish the look using what I actually own?”

Most outfit apps start from zero: upload a photo, get a full recommendation. But getting dressed often starts differently. You’ve already picked your jeans, your shoes, or a blazer for dinner. You don’t need AI to reinvent those pieces — you need it to respect your choices and fill in the gaps.

So we built “Complete an outfit from selected wardrobe pieces.”

The idea: select 1–5 items (shirt, trousers, blazer, shoes, belt), set occasion, season, style, and notes, then tap Complete outfit with AI. Your picks stay locked in; the AI suggests only what’s missing.

How we refined it (from real screenshots and feedback):

→ One item per slot — no double shirts
→ Preferences inline on Wardrobe — no tab-hopping to set occasion or style
→ Selection thumbnails — confirm your picks before you generate

Under the hood: the backend pins your selected items to the right outfit slots and tells the model: keep these pieces, complete the rest. One item per slot, 1–5 items max, category aliases (polo → shirt, jeans → trousers), ownership checks — then AI fills only the empty slots.

Why I love it: it mirrors how I actually get dressed. I start with an anchor piece — “these shoes for this dinner” — and build outward. Same flow on web and iOS.

Try it: https://sajadparacha.github.io/outfit-suggestor-app/

Sign in, add wardrobe items, select a couple, set Dinner / Night Out + Winter + Classic, and hit Complete outfit with AI.

The best AI features aren’t always the flashiest models — they’re the ones that fit how people already behave.
